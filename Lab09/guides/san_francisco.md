# San Francisco, California — Travel Guide

San Francisco is a compact city of about 7 miles by 7 miles, built on steep hills overlooking the San Francisco Bay. Despite its small size, it is densely packed with neighborhoods that each have a distinct character — from the tech-heavy South of Market (SoMa) to the colorful Victorian houses of the Painted Ladies near Alamo Square.

## Best Season

September and October are the best months. The famous summer fog (locals call it "Karl the Fog") rolls in from the Pacific and keeps June and July surprisingly cold — often under 60°F even at midday. Pack layers regardless of when you visit. Winters are mild and rainy.

## Food

San Francisco's food scene is exceptional. Must-try experiences:
- **Sourdough bread** from Boudin Bakery at Fisherman's Wharf — San Francisco's wild yeast starter dates back to the Gold Rush era
- **Dim sum in the Richmond District** — try Ton Kiang or Hong Kong Lounge for weekend brunch
- **Burritos in the Mission District** — La Taqueria on Mission Street is consistently rated among the best in the city
- **Ferry Building Marketplace** on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday mornings for local farmers market produce and artisan food stalls

## Activities

- Walk across the **Golden Gate Bridge** from the south side — the round trip is about 4 miles and free
- Explore **Alcatraz Island** — book audio tour tickets at least 2 weeks in advance, they sell out
- Take a vintage **cable car** on the Powell-Hyde line down to Fisherman's Wharf (not just a tourist gimmick — it's actual city transit)
- **Golden Gate Park** has free museums, a Japanese Tea Garden, and buffalo paddock — much larger and quieter than it looks on a map

## Practical Tips

The Bay Area has a regional transit card called **Clipper** — load it at any BART station and it works on BART, Muni buses, cable cars, and ferries. Rideshare apps are popular but surges during commute hours can be expensive. The city has a significant unhoused population concentrated in the Tenderloin and Civic Center areas — be aware of your surroundings in those neighborhoods.

## Budget

Mid-range budget: $120–$200 per day. Hotel prices are high — consider staying in the Inner Richmond or Outer Sunset neighborhoods for lower rates with easy transit access to downtown.
