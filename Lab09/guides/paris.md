# Paris, France — Travel Guide

Paris is divided into 20 arrondissements (districts) arranged in a clockwise spiral from the center. The city is very walkable — many major attractions are within 30–40 minutes on foot from each other. The Métro is efficient, clean, and runs until around 1am (2am on weekends).

## Best Season

April to June and September to October offer mild weather, fewer crowds than peak summer, and lower hotel prices. July and August bring heavy tourist crowds and some locals leave the city for vacation, causing some smaller restaurants and shops to close. Christmas markets in December are charming but cold.

## Food

French cuisine has precise regional traditions. In Paris:
- **Croissants and pain au chocolat** — buy from a boulangerie (bakery), not a supermarket chain. Look for the "Artisan Boulanger" sign
- **Steak frites** — the classic Parisian bistro meal. Entrecôte (ribeye) is typically served medium-rare unless you specify otherwise
- **Crêpes** from street stands near tourist areas are cheap and filling — go for savory galettes (buckwheat crêpes with ham, egg, and cheese) for a real meal
- **Wine and cheese picnic** — buy from a fromagerie and a cave à vins, then eat in the Champ de Mars or Luxembourg Gardens

## Activities

- **Musée d'Orsay** houses the world's best Impressionist collection (Monet, Renoir, Degas) — less crowded than the Louvre and free on the first Sunday of each month
- **Walk along the Seine** from Notre-Dame to the Eiffel Tower — about 4km, passing Shakespeare and Company bookstore
- **Montmartre neighborhood** — Sacré-Cœur basilica at the top has a panoramic view; the surrounding streets have artists and cafés
- **Versailles** day trip (45 min by RER C train) — arrive before 10am to beat tour groups

## Practical Tips

A **Carnet** (book of 10 Métro tickets) is cheaper than buying single tickets. Tipping is not required but rounding up the bill is appreciated. Many Paris museums are free for visitors under 26 from EU countries — check eligibility before paying. Most restaurants do not open for dinner before 7pm.

## Budget

Paris is moderately expensive. Mid-range budget: €120–€180 per day including a 3-star hotel, two sit-down meals, and admissions. Picnic lunches from markets significantly reduce food costs.
