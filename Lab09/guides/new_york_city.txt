New York City, USA — Travel Guide

New York City is made up of five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Most tourists spend the majority of their time in Manhattan and Brooklyn. The subway runs 24 hours a day and is the fastest way to get around — a single ride costs $2.90 with a MetroCard or tap-to-pay credit card.

Best Season: Spring (April–May) and fall (September–October) offer mild weather and fewer extreme crowds. Summer is busy and hot. December brings holiday markets and ice skating at Bryant Park and Rockefeller Center, but prices spike significantly.

Food: New York is famous for its pizza — a classic dollar slice from a neighborhood pizzeria beats most sit-down restaurants. Bagels with lox (smoked salmon) and cream cheese are a local staple, best from Ess-a-Bagel or Murray's Bagels. The Meatpacking District and Lower East Side have trendy restaurant scenes. For cheap and good food, explore Flushing, Queens for authentic Chinese and Korean cuisine, or Jackson Heights for South Asian food.

Activities: Walk the High Line (elevated park on an old freight rail line) from Gansevoort Street to 34th Street. Visit the Brooklyn Bridge and walk across for free. The Metropolitan Museum of Art (Met) suggests a donation but entry is pay-what-you-wish for NY state residents. Central Park is free and great for a morning run or picnic.

Practical Tips: Many museums are free on certain days or evenings — check their websites before paying full price. Yellow cabs and Uber/Lyft are expensive; stick to the subway. Street parking is nearly impossible — don't rent a car in the city.

Budget: NYC is expensive. Expect $150–$250 per day for mid-range travel including a hostel or budget hotel, meals, and transit.
